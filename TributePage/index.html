Biography

Ratan Tata was an Indian industrialist and philanthropist. He served as the chairman of Tata Group and Tata Sons from 1991 to 2012 and he held the position of interim chairman from October 2016 to February 2017. In 2000, he received the Padma Bhushan, the third highest civilian honour in India, followed by the Padma Vibhushan, the country's second highest civilian honour, in 2008.

Ratan Tata is one of India’s most influential business leaders, known for his leadership of the Tata Group, which transformed the company into a global conglomerate. Under his stewardship, Tata expanded into sectors such as steel, automobiles, IT, and hospitality, with some key acquisitions like Tetley Tea and Jaguar Land Rover.

Ratan Tata's legacy is one of integrity, visionary leadership, and dedication to making a positive impact on society. Under his leadership, Tata Group grew into one of the largest and most respected conglomerates in the world. Ratan Tata’s business acumen, coupled with his commitment to the betterment of society, has made him a respected figure not only in India but globally.

He is considered one of the most influential business leaders in India and is admired for his ethical approach to business and his relentless efforts to bring about positive change in the country.
